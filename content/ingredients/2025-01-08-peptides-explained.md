# Peptides in Skincare: What Works

Peptides are everywhere in anti-aging skincare. But with hundreds of peptides on the market, how do you know which ones actually work?

## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids‚Äîthe building blocks of proteins like collagen and elastin. In skincare, they act as signaling molecules, telling your skin to produce more of these structural proteins.

Think of them as text messages to your skin cells: "Hey, make more collagen!"

**What they do:** Stimulate fibroblasts to produce collagen, elastin, and other matrix proteins.

**Key players:**

## The GHK-Cu Story

Copper peptide (GHK-Cu) is the most researched peptide in skincare with over 70 published studies. It's been shown to:

- Increase collagen production by 70%
- Improve skin elasticity by 40%
- Accelerate wound healing
- Reduce inflammation

The catch? It's expensive and challenging to formulate. Look for products from reputable brands with stable formulations.

## Realistic Expectations

Let's be clear: peptides are not magic. They won't:

- Replace Botox or fillers
- Reverse 20 years of aging overnight
- Work if used inconsistently

They will:

- Provide modest improvement over 2-3 months
- Support overall skin health
- Complement other anti-aging strategies
